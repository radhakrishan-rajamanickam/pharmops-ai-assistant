1. PURPOSE
This SOP defines how PharmaCo communicates with suppliers 
during quality events, deviations, certification issues, and 
routine operational matters. It ensures consistent, documented, 
and timely communication that protects PharmaCo's regulatory 
standing and supplier relationships.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all communication between PharmaCo procurement, 
quality, and operations teams and external suppliers.

3. COMMUNICATION CHANNELS AND HIERARCHY
3.1 Operational queries: Email to supplier account manager.
3.2 Quality deviations: Formal written notification via 
Supplier Portal within defined timeframes.
3.3 GxP certification issues: Registered email to supplier 
Quality Director with read receipt required.
3.4 Suspension notifications: Phone call followed by formal 
written notice within 2 hours.
3.5 Emergency supply issues: Phone call to supplier 
emergency contact, followed by email within 1 hour.

4. NOTIFICATION TIMEFRAMES
4.1 Cold chain deviation involving supplier materials: 
Notify within 2 hours of deviation detection (SOP-QA-0042).
4.2 PO deviation identified at goods receipt: 
Notify within 8 hours of identification (SOP-SC-0031).
4.3 GxP certificate expiry approaching (90 days): 
Send requalification notice via Supplier Portal.
4.4 GxP certificate lapsed — suspension: 
Phone call within 4 hours, written notice within 2 hours 
of phone call.
4.5 Quality Hold on supplier materials: 
Notify within 24 hours with Hold reference number.

5. REQUIRED CONTENT FOR QUALITY NOTIFICATIONS
All formal quality notifications to suppliers must include:
5.1 PharmaCo site name and site code (SITE-A or SITE-B).
5.2 Supplier ID as registered in PharmaCo Supplier Master.
5.3 Affected Purchase Order number(s).
5.4 Nature and classification of the deviation or issue.
5.5 Materials affected with batch numbers where available.
5.6 Required supplier response and deadline.
5.7 Name and contact details of PharmaCo responsible person.

6. CAPA REQUEST PROCEDURE
6.1 For Major and Critical deviations, PharmaCo must 
formally request a CAPA from the supplier.
6.2 CAPA request must be sent within 5 business days of 
deviation classification.
6.3 Supplier must acknowledge CAPA request within 48 hours.
6.4 Supplier must submit completed CAPA within 30 days.
6.5 PharmaCo Quality team reviews and approves or rejects 
supplier CAPA within 10 business days.
6.6 Rejected CAPAs require resubmission within 15 days.

7. ESCALATION PROCEDURE
7.1 If supplier does not acknowledge within required timeframe, 
escalate to Procurement Manager.
7.2 If supplier does not respond within 5 business days of 
escalation, escalate to Quality Director.
7.3 Persistent non-response triggers qualification review 
per SOP-SC-0018.

8. DOCUMENTATION
All supplier communications related to quality events must 
be logged in the Supplier Communication Register on the 
day of communication. Records retained for 7 years.

9. FIRST ACTION CHECKLIST FOR COMMON SCENARIOS
Scenario A — GxP cert lapsed, open PO exists:
  1. Place PO on Quality Hold immediately
  2. Call supplier Quality Director
  3. Send formal suspension notice within 2 hours
  4. Notify site Operations Manager within 4 hours
  5. Initiate CAPA within 5 business days

Scenario B — Cold chain deviation, supplier materials affected:
  1. Place materials on Quality Hold
  2. Notify supplier within 2 hours
  3. Request temperature data from supplier
  4. Complete deviation report within 24 hours
  5. Request supplier CAPA if Major or Critical

Scenario C — PO quantity deviation greater than 20%:
  1. Refuse excess or shortage at goods receipt
  2. Notify supplier within 8 hours
  3. Raise Critical PO Deviation in procurement system
  4. Request root cause analysis within 5 business days
  5. Quality Director sign-off required for resolution
