Version: 2.1
Status: ACTIVE

Owner: Site Quality Manager
Steward: Quality Operations Lead
Last Reviewed: 2025-12-01
Next Review: 2026-12-01

1. PURPOSE
This SOP defines the quality and safety requirements for 
PharmaCo manufacturing sites, with specific provisions for 
Site Alpha and Site Beta operations. It ensures all site 
activities comply with GxP regulations and PharmaCo global 
quality standards.

2. SITE QUALITY REQUIREMENTS
2.1 All manufacturing activities at Site Alpha and Site Beta 
must comply with current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP).
2.2 Site quality plans must be reviewed and approved annually 
by the Site Quality Manager and Regional Quality Director.
2.3 Internal quality audits must be conducted quarterly.
2.4 All critical quality events must be reported to the 
Global Quality team within 24 hours.

3. SUPPLIER ACCESS TO SITES
3.1 Only Approved suppliers per SOP-SC-0018 may deliver 
materials directly to Site Alpha or Site Beta.
3.2 Supplier delivery personnel must sign site visitor log 
and complete site safety briefing before entry.
3.3 Suspended suppliers are not permitted on site. Any 
attempted delivery from a Suspended supplier must be refused 
and reported to the Procurement Lead immediately.

4. QUALITY HOLD AREAS
4.1 Each site must maintain a designated Quality Hold area 
that is physically separated from approved stock.
4.2 Quality Hold areas must be secured and access restricted 
to Quality Officers only.
4.3 Inventory system must reflect Quality Hold status in 
real time.

5. DEVIATION MANAGEMENT AT SITE LEVEL
5.1 Site operators must report any quality deviation 
immediately to the site Quality Officer.
5.2 Site Quality Officer has authority to place materials 
on Quality Hold without prior approval.
5.3 All site deviations must be assessed for CAPA requirement 
within 5 business days.
5.4 Repeat deviations of the same type within 90 days trigger 
mandatory escalation to Regional Quality Director.

6. CAPA MANAGEMENT
6.1 CAPA stands for Corrective and Preventive Action.
6.2 Corrective action addresses the immediate cause of a 
deviation.
6.3 Preventive action addresses the root cause to prevent 
recurrence.
6.4 All CAPAs must be assigned an owner and a target 
completion date within 5 business days of initiation.
6.5 CAPAs must be reviewed monthly by the site Quality team.
6.6 Overdue CAPAs must be escalated to the Quality Director.

7. REGULATORY INSPECTION READINESS
7.1 Sites must maintain inspection readiness at all times.
7.2 All SOPs must be current — no SOP may be used if past 
its review date.
7.3 Temperature records, deviation logs, and CAPA registers 
must be available for inspection within 2 hours of request.
7.4 Supplier qualification records including GxP certificates 
must be maintained and accessible for all active suppliers.
