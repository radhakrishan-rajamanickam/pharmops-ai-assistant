1. PURPOSE
This SOP defines how Purchase Order deviations are identified, 
classified, approved, and resolved at PharmaCo manufacturing sites. 
A PO deviation occurs when a delivered order differs from the 
agreed specification in quantity, quality, timing, or documentation.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all purchase orders raised by PharmaCo sites for 
direct materials, indirect materials, and contract services.

3. DEVIATION CLASSIFICATION
3.1 Minor PO Deviation
Quantity variance less than 5% of ordered amount.
Documentation minor omission correctable within 48 hours.
No GxP impact. Resolved by Procurement Officer.

3.2 Major PO Deviation
Quantity variance between 5% and 20%.
Quality specification outside agreed tolerance.
Delivery date more than 5 business days late.
Requires Procurement Manager approval and supplier CAPA.

3.3 Critical PO Deviation
Quantity variance greater than 20%.
Material fails quality testing.
Supplier GxP certification not current at time of delivery.
Requires Quality Director sign-off and regulatory notification 
if applicable.

4. RESPONSE PROCEDURE
Step 1: Receiving team identifies deviation at goods receipt 
and raises Deviation Notification in procurement system.
Step 2: Procurement Officer classifies deviation within 4 hours.
Step 3: Affected materials placed on Quality Hold pending 
classification review.
Step 4: Supplier notified per SOP-OPS-0007 within 8 hours 
of deviation identification.
Step 5: For Major and Critical deviations, supplier must 
submit root cause analysis within 5 business days.
Step 6: Procurement Manager reviews root cause and approves 
or rejects supplier response.
Step 7: CAPA agreed and tracked to closure.

5. QUALITY HOLD MANAGEMENT
5.1 Materials on Quality Hold must be physically segregated 
and clearly labelled.
5.2 Quality Hold status must be reflected in inventory system 
within 2 hours of decision.
5.3 No materials on Quality Hold may be used in production.
5.4 Quality Hold may only be released by the Quality Officer 
with written authorisation.

6. SUPPLIER IMPACT ASSESSMENT
For any Critical PO Deviation, the Supply Chain team must 
assess the supplier's qualification status per SOP-SC-0018. 
If the deviation is linked to a GxP certification issue, 
immediate suspension procedure applies.

7. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
All PO deviations must be logged in the procurement system 
on the day of identification. Major and Critical deviations 
require formal Deviation Report within 24 hours. Records 
retained for 7 years minimum.
