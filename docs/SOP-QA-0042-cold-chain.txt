1. PURPOSE
This SOP defines the requirements for monitoring cold chain 
temperature conditions during storage and transportation of 
temperature-sensitive pharmaceutical materials at PharmaCo 
manufacturing sites. It establishes procedures for detecting, 
documenting, and responding to temperature deviations to ensure 
product quality and GxP compliance.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all temperature-sensitive raw materials, intermediates, 
and finished products handled at Site Alpha and Site Beta. Includes 
storage areas, transport vehicles, and loading/unloading zones.

3. TEMPERATURE REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Refrigerated storage: +2°C to +8°C
3.2 Controlled room temperature: +15°C to +25°C
3.3 Frozen storage: -20°C ± 5°C
3.4 Ultra-low temperature: -70°C ± 10°C

4. MONITORING REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Continuous electronic temperature monitoring is mandatory for 
all cold chain storage areas.
4.2 Data loggers must be calibrated every 12 months.
4.3 Alarms must be set at 1°C from the acceptable limit to allow 
response before a deviation occurs.
4.4 Temperature records must be reviewed daily by the designated 
site quality officer.

5. DEVIATION DETECTION AND CLASSIFICATION
5.1 Minor deviation: Temperature outside range for less than 
30 minutes. No immediate product impact expected.
5.2 Major deviation: Temperature outside range for 30 minutes 
to 4 hours. Product impact assessment required.
5.3 Critical deviation: Temperature outside range for more than 
4 hours. Immediate product quarantine and quality hold required.

6. RESPONSE PROCEDURE FOR TEMPERATURE DEVIATION
Step 1: Upon detection of deviation, the site operator must 
immediately acknowledge the alarm and document the time.
Step 2: Attempt to restore correct temperature conditions within 
15 minutes.
Step 3: Notify the site Quality Officer within 30 minutes of 
detection regardless of deviation classification.
Step 4: For Major and Critical deviations, notify the supplier 
within 2 hours using the Supplier Communication Protocol 
(SOP-OPS-0007).
Step 5: Place affected materials on Quality Hold in the inventory 
system pending impact assessment.
Step 6: Complete Deviation Report Form QA-DEV-001 within 24 hours.
Step 7: Initiate CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) for all 
Major and Critical deviations within 5 business days.

7. SUPPLIER NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
7.1 If the deviation involves materials recently received from a 
supplier, the supplier must be notified per SOP-OPS-0007.
7.2 Suppliers with active GxP certification must acknowledge 
notification within 24 hours.
7.3 Suppliers without current GxP certification must be escalated 
immediately to the Procurement Lead for qualification review per 
SOP-SC-0018.

8. DOCUMENTATION
All temperature deviation events must be recorded in the Quality 
Management System within 24 hours. Records must be retained for 
a minimum of 7 years. CAPA records must be linked to the original 
deviation report.
